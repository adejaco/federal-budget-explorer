Data Sources

All data used in the U.S. 2025 Federal Budget Explorer — primary government sources only.

Budget Totals & Historical Spending

OMB Historical Tables — Table 1.1: Summary of Receipts, Outlays, and Surpluses or Deficits
Primary source for all annual totals. Used for total outlays, revenues, and deficit/surplus by fiscal year (1962–2025).
whitehouse.gov/omb/budget/historical-tables
OMB Historical Tables — Table 3.2: Outlays by Function and Subfunction
Spending by major category (defense, Social Security, Medicare, Income Security, Federal Retirement, etc.) going back to 1962.
whitehouse.gov/omb/budget/historical-tables
OMB Historical Tables — Table 4.1: Outlays by Agency
Agency-level historical spending (VA, HHS, DoEd, DoT, DHS) going back to 1962.
whitehouse.gov/omb/budget/historical-tables
OMB Historical Tables — Table 7.1: Federal Debt at End of Year
Total federal debt outstanding 1940–2025. Used for the debt overlay on the Net Interest trend chart.
whitehouse.gov/omb/budget/historical-tables

Budget Projections

CBO Budget & Economic Outlook, February 2026
Primary source for all 10-year projections (2026–2036). Incorporates effects of the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBB, July 2025).
cbo.gov/publication/budget-economic-outlook
CBO Long-Term Budget Outlook
Longer-horizon % of GDP projections and structural deficit analysis.
cbo.gov/topics/budget/long-term-budget-projections

Revenue & Tax Distribution

OMB Historical Tables — Tables 2.1–2.5: Receipts by Source
Federal revenue by type (individual income, payroll, corporate, excise, tariffs) going back to 1934.
whitehouse.gov/omb/budget/historical-tables
IRS Statistics of Income (SOI) — Tax Year 2023 [TY2023 — most recent available]
Individual income tax distribution by percentile: share of AGI, share of taxes paid, effective rates. Typically 2 years behind the current fiscal year. Used for "Who Pays?" panels.
irs.gov/statistics/soi-tax-stats-individual-income-tax-returns
CBO Distribution of Federal Taxes, 2022
Payroll tax incidence by income quintile including effective rates. Used for "Who Pays?" payroll panel.
cbo.gov/publication/distribution-household-income
IRS SOI Publication 16 — Corporation Income Tax Returns, Tax Year 2022
Corporate income tax liability by industry sector. Scaled to FY2025 actuals.
irs.gov/statistics/soi-tax-stats-corporation-tax-statistics

Notes on Data

Payroll Tax Coverage
All payroll tax figures include both the employee and employer share of FICA contributions (Social Security: 6.2% each = 12.4% combined; Medicare: 1.45% each = 2.9% combined). Employers remit both shares to the IRS.
Fiscal Year Convention
The U.S. federal fiscal year runs October 1 – September 30. A new president inherits ~9 months of the prior administration's budget framework in their first year.
Projection Methodology
All projections use CBO's February 2026 baseline, incorporating enacted legislation including the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (signed July 2025). Projections are baselines, not forecasts.
Rounding
All figures rounded to nearest billion dollars. Subtotals may not add to totals due to rounding.
